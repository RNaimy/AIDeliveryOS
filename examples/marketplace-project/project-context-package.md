## Executive Summary  
TradeLink Marketplace aims to build a vertical marketplace connecting commercial property owners with qualified investors seeking off-market opportunities. By focusing on a niche segment, the platform intends to solve supply acquisition and demand liquidity challenges through a trusted, secure environment. The MVP will validate supply-demand matching mechanics and user engagement before scaling. This approach mitigates early-stage risks and ensures alignment with market needs.

---

## Problem Statement  
Commercial property owners often struggle to find qualified investors for off-market deals, while investors lack access to exclusive opportunities. Existing platforms are either too broad or lack trust mechanisms, resulting in inefficient discovery and matching.

---

## Context Gap Statement  
Current solutions do not adequately address the unique challenges of off-market commercial property transactions, including trust, verification, and liquidity. There is a need for a focused marketplace that reduces friction and accelerates deal flow.

## Business Case  
TradeLink Marketplace will generate revenue through a hybrid model of subscription fees for premium access, lead generation commissions, and transaction fees. The platform’s focus on off-market opportunities creates a defensible niche with high-value network effects.

## Business Model Evaluation

| Model | Advantages | Risks | MVP Fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Subscription | Predictable recurring revenue | Requires ongoing value delivery | High |
| Lead Generation | Fast to launch | Lower revenue ceiling | High |
| Transaction Fees | Strong long-term upside | Requires transaction volume | Medium |
| Hybrid Model | Diversified revenue streams | Operational complexity | High |

### Recommended Approach

Launch with a hybrid model focused on subscriptions and lead generation.

Defer transaction-based monetization until marketplace liquidity has been validated.

## Technology Guidance  

### Architecture Principle

Prefer operational simplicity over technical sophistication until marketplace liquidity has been validated.

- Leverage existing marketplace platforms or no-code tools for rapid MVP assembly.  
- Use modular, API-driven architecture to enable future scaling.  
- Integrate third-party verification services to minimize development overhead.  
- Avoid building complex custom features before validation.

## Technology Decision Rationale  
Using existing tools accelerates time-to-market and reduces technical risk. API-driven modularity supports iterative development and scaling based on validated needs.
